Indian classical music has its roots in the Vedas of Hinduism. The tradition of classical music continued in the Delhi Sultanate era too. The traditions then acquired distinct two forms.

North Indian music, also called Hindustani music, and South Indian music also called Carnatic music are those two forms.

Ustad Amir Khan, Ustad Bade Ghulam Ali Khan, Ustad Bismillah Khan are some of the stalwarts of Hindustani music while Pt. M.Balamuralikrishna, L. Subramaniam are some of the stalwarts of Carnatic music.
